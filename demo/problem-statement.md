# Problem Statement

## Core Problem

Job seekers are being asked to trust job posts, recruiter messages, and "remote work" offers before they can verify who is behind them. The risky posts often look ordinary at first: a real-looking company name, a vague role, a high salary, and a fast next step through Telegram, Messenger, Viber, WhatsApp, or another off-platform channel.

## Evidence

This is a real and current problem, not a hypothetical one.

- The FTC reported that U.S. job-scam losses more than tripled from 2020 to 2023 and exceeded $220 million in the first half of 2024 alone.
- The FTC also estimated that task scams made up 38.8% of job-scam reports in the first half of 2024, up from 5.6% in 2023.
- The UN Philippines warned in June 2025 about fake job vacancies impersonating UN entities, including vague job descriptions, upfront fees, false urgency, and realistic-looking documents.
- BusinessWorld reported that the Philippine SEC warned job-seeking Filipinos about job-offer scams on Viber, Messenger, and Telegram, including "tasking and recharging" schemes that require deposits before victims can withdraw supposed earnings.
- The FBI warns that fake work-from-home and cryptocurrency job scams can push victims into sending money before they realize the job is fraudulent.

## User Pain

The first users are students, fresh graduates, career switchers, junior professionals, and remote-work applicants. They are often applying under pressure and may not have enough experience to spot weak company signals, unrealistic compensation, fake recruiter identities, or unsafe payment requests.

## Current Behavior

Today, verification is manual. A careful applicant has to search the company, check news, inspect the URL, compare salary and role details, look for local presence, and decide whether the recruiter contact method is normal. That takes several tabs and still ends in a judgment call.

Most users do one of three things:

1. Ignore the post and maybe miss a real opportunity.
2. Apply anyway and hope it is legitimate.
3. Spend too much time manually checking every signal.

## Why Existing Tools Do Not Solve It

Search engines return pages, not verdicts. Job boards show listings, not investigation trails. Chatbots can give generic advice, but they often do not show what evidence was checked. Scam-awareness articles teach red flags, but they do not evaluate the specific post in front of the user.

## Product Opportunity

HireProof turns a scattered verification process into one visible investigation.

The user pastes a job post, recruiter message, or job URL. The agent extracts the claims, checks live evidence, compares similar jobs, looks for company footprint, and returns a structured report:

- **Safe**, **Caution**, or **High-Risk** verdict
- risk score
- extracted claims
- red flags and green flags
- evidence cards with source links
- safer alternatives
- next steps

## One-Sentence Problem

Job seekers need a faster way to verify suspicious opportunities before they apply, because manual checking is slow, scattered, and easy to skip.

## One-Sentence Solution

HireProof is a job-post investigator that checks live evidence and returns a proof-backed risk verdict before the user applies.

## Design Principle

The product should not feel like a generic AI chat assistant. It should feel like a serious investigation panel: fast, clear, sourced, and careful about uncertainty.
