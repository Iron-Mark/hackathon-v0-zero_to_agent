Last updated: 2026-05-10, Asia/Manila

## Best First Targets

1. Career communities with Discord, Facebook, Slack, or Telegram job channels.
2. Schools, bootcamps, and student orgs with internship or entry-level job posts.
3. Recruiters or job boards that want a lightweight trust check for questionable posts.
4. Builders who want to test the API, MCP, or automation flow with their own provider keys.

## Qualification Rubric

- Strong fit: has repeated suspicious job posts, one clear moderation or review workflow, and a person willing to test real examples.
- Medium fit: interested in job safety but has no current examples or clear workflow.
- Weak fit: wants broad fraud detection, guaranteed detection, or unrelated security coverage.

## Success Signals

- They send real examples without heavy prompting.
- They ask for exports, API access, webhooks, or repeat checks.
- They report a false positive or missed signal with enough detail to improve the workflow.
- They are willing to bring provider credentials for live checks.

## Guardrails

- Do not promise guaranteed scam detection.
- Do not pitch it as a generic fraud platform.
- Do not offer unlimited public live-provider usage.
- Keep the pilot framed as evidence support for safer job-seeking decisions.
